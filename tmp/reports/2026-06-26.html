A Brand for Every Customer Document

New This Period · The Big One

The gap. Jeetaw sells precision vacuum components to Applied Materials and aerospace customers — the kind of buyers who judge a supplier partly on how its paperwork looks. Until now, the documents that left the building were styled ad-hoc, with no shared look.

What we built. A complete brand system — colors, typography, logos, and reusable parts (85 files in all) — plus finished, on-brand templates for the six documents Jeetaw produces:

SalesQuote, Order Confirmation, and Packing Slip — what the customer receives.
MoneyInvoice and Purchase Order — billing and buying, consistent at last.
ShopShop Traveler — the job sheet that follows a part through the floor.
OfficeBranded letterhead and memo for the front office.

Why it matters. Every customer touchpoint now looks like one company — polished, consistent, and quick to produce. For a precision shop, that look is part of the product.

Honest status — the templates are designed and sitting on the company file server. Rolling them into day-to-day QuickBooks use is the next step.
02

A Full Employee Safety-Training Program

New This Period

The need. A California shop that works with welding, lasers, and compressed gases is required to run documented safety training. Jeetaw needed a real program — not a binder of borrowed PDFs.

What we built. An annual training plan plus ten training modules, each pre-filled with Jeetaw's real facility and chemical-inventory details:

CoversInjury-prevention plan, protective equipment & hearing, safe lifting, TIG welding & hot work, laser safety, power tools, hazardous materials, chemical labeling, emergency response, and incident reporting.
GroundedBuilt on Jeetaw's actual gas inventory and facility ID — not generic boilerplate.

Why it matters. It keeps the shop compliant and audit-ready with state safety rules — and, more importantly, it keeps people safe. It also lowers the company's regulatory and liability risk.

Honest status — a few shop-specific details are left as quick fill-in blanks; from there it prints as a signed, ready-to-use program.

Future-Proofing the Company's Knowledge

Infrastructure

Jeetaw keeps a large written knowledge base — how the network runs, customer history, part details, and past decisions. We set it up to export itself automatically every night into an open, standard format, with an interactive map you can open in any web browser.

Why it matters. The company's hard-won know-how is now backed up, portable, and easy to navigate — instead of locked inside one app. It's one of the business's real assets, treated like one.
