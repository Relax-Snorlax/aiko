The Automatic Quoting Tool

New This Period · The Big One

The problem. Jeetaw gets a constant stream of requests-for-quote from Applied Materials. Each one takes about 30 minutes of an expert reading drawings and 3D models, working out the tubes, bends, and welds needed — then pricing it by hand.

What we built. Software that opens the customer's 3D model file and automatically detects the tubes (count and lengths), the bends, and the welds — and flags parts that can't be built. The slow, expensive part of quoting, now done in a fraction of a second.

How we proved it. We tested it against 4,214 real past quotes. Its price landed within 20% of what a human actually charged on 83% of them — good enough to use as a reviewed first draft.

Honest status: we built the engine, not the whole car. It works and it's proven — but it doesn't have a point-and-click screen yet, so it isn't in daily use. Next up: a simple web interface and one final data-reading step.
02

The Parts Catalog Overhaul

Major Progress

The catalog. Jeetaw keeps a digital catalog of every fitting it uses. The catalog app itself was built fresh this period — about 3,748 parts.

The gap we found. It was missing prices on roughly 92% of parts — essentially a catalog with no price tags.

The fix came from two places:

Source 1 Every old quote spreadsheet secretly held our own price list. We recovered ~734,000 price data points from 7,840 old quotes.
Source 2 We connected to QuickBooks and pulled 2 years of purchase orders — 459 of them — for accurate supplier costs from Banner, Swagelok, Hytron and others.

The result. The catalog went from almost no pricing to having real supplier costs and sale prices on hundreds of parts — including the specialized Hytron modular fittings used constantly in assemblies. Everything is documented so the work can continue.
